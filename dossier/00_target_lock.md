## 🔷 Level Risk — the most important unknown

Level is unresolved, and it is the field that most changes the bar. Amazon publishes level codes inconsistently in job postings, and entry-level scientist titles differ by org and geography. **This course does not guess a level code.**

**Consequence, stated plainly:** every "the bar expects…" statement in Chapter 1 and every Company-Relative score in Chapter 2 carries a ⚠️ marker until level is confirmed. Scores computed against an assumed level are directionally useful and numerically unreliable.

**Action:** ask the recruiter, or read the level from the posting when you apply, then re-run Chapter 2's Company-Relative score.

---

## 🔷 What Being Mid-Degree Changes

You are applying **~12 months before you can start**. Three consequences most candidates miss:

1. **You are interviewing against your July 2027 self, but with your July 2026 evidence.** Everything you build between now and application strengthens the resume the loop actually sees. This is the single largest lever you hold, and it expires.
2. **The "no industry experience" gap is time-limited.** A 2027 internship (summer 2027 is likely too late for a 2027 start, but a 2026–27 research collaboration or lab publication is not) can still change the profile. See Chapter 5.
3. **Timeline is long — which is a trap.** ~12 months feels infinite, so preparation drifts. The Chapter 5 plan is paced against application season, not the interview date.

⚠️ **Assumption flagged:** "full-time 2027 start" assumes you apply in the standard 2026–27 cycle. If IIT Guwahati placement season governs your timeline instead, the application date may be much earlier than the start date — confirm with your placement cell, because it compresses Chapter 5 substantially.

## 🟢 Confirm-With-Recruiter Checklist

Carry this into your first recruiter conversation. Answers unlock the ⚠️ markers throughout the course.

```markdown
- [ ] What level is this role, and what does the bar look like at that level?
- [ ] How many rounds, in what order, and how long is each?
- [ ] Is there an ML system design round for this level?
- [ ] Which language(s) are permitted in the coding round?
- [ ] Which team/org, and what problem space?
- [ ] Is the timeline tied to campus placement season or rolling applications?
- [ ] Are publications expected or advantageous for this role?
- [ ] Who conducts the behavioural round?
```

**When you get answers:** update this table, then re-run Chapter 2's Company-Relative score and Chapter 5's ordering. Both are conditional on this page.
